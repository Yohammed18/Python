Writing a requirement document for an e-commerce site for a hair salon involves clearly defining the features and functionality needed to meet the goals of the business and its users. Here's a breakdown of the requirements you could include:

1. Purpose of the E-Commerce Site
To provide an online platform for customers to book appointments, purchase hair care products, and learn about the salon's services.
To enhance customer convenience and streamline salon operations.
2. User Roles and Permissions
2.1 Customers
Browse services and products.
Create an account or log in.
Book appointments and view booking history.
Purchase products online.
Leave reviews and feedback.
Receive notifications (e.g., appointment reminders, promotional offers).
Manage their profile (name, contact details, payment methods).
2.2 Salon Staff
View and manage appointments.
Update service availability.
Process and fulfill product orders.
View and respond to customer reviews.
2.3 Administrators
Manage services, products, and staff accounts.
Configure promotional campaigns (e.g., discounts, bundles).
Access analytics for appointments, sales, and customer feedback.
3. Functional Requirements
3.1 Home Page
Display a welcoming message and a featured banner (e.g., current promotions).
Showcase popular services and products.
Include a search bar for services/products.
3.2 Service Booking
Display a list of services with descriptions, prices, and durations.
Allow users to select a service, view available slots, and book an appointment.
Enable rescheduling or canceling appointments.
Provide real-time availability based on the salon's schedule.
3.3 Product Catalog
List products with images, descriptions, prices, and stock status.
Allow users to add products to a cart and checkout.
Filter and sort products by category, price, or popularity.
Enable customer reviews and ratings for products.
3.4 Checkout
Support multiple payment methods (e.g., credit/debit cards, digital wallets).
Allow users to apply promotional codes.
Provide an order summary before payment.
Send confirmation emails for purchases and appointments.
3.5 User Accounts
Enable user registration and login (via email or social login).
Allow users to view and edit their profiles.
Provide a history of bookings and orders.
3.6 Notifications
Email and SMS notifications for appointment confirmations, reminders, and order updates.
Push notifications for promotions and updates (optional, if a mobile app is planned).
3.7 Admin Dashboard
Manage services, staff schedules, and availability.
Add, update, or remove products and categories.
Monitor sales and bookings through analytics and reports.
Create and manage discount codes or promotions.
4. Non-Functional Requirements
Performance: Pages should load within 2 seconds.
Scalability: Handle high traffic during promotions or peak hours.
Security: Implement SSL encryption, secure payments, and data protection measures.
Accessibility: Comply with accessibility standards (e.g., WCAG 2.1).
Mobile Responsiveness: The site should work seamlessly on desktops, tablets, and smartphones.
5. Integration Requirements
Payment gateways (e.g., PayPal, Stripe).
Appointment scheduling APIs (if using third-party tools).
Email/SMS services for notifications.
Optional: Integration with salon management software.
6. Future Enhancements
Loyalty program for frequent customers.
Virtual consultation for personalized service/product recommendations.
Integration with social media for sharing promotions or reviews.
7. Assumptions and Constraints
The salon has an existing list of services and products ready for the site.
The site will initially support one physical location.
All images and content will be provided by the salon.
